# Opening concepts

## Central control

Controlling central squares gives pieces more useful routes and limits the opponent's activity. Pawn moves such as e4, d4, e5, and d5 can occupy the center, while pieces can control it from nearby squares.

## Development

Opening development brings knights and bishops toward active squares. Moving the same piece repeatedly without a concrete reason can leave the rest of the army undeveloped.

## King safety

Castling usually moves the king away from the center and connects the rooks. A player should avoid opening lines toward an uncastled king unless there is a concrete tactical justification.

## Pawn tension

When opposing pawns attack each other, either side may capture, advance, or maintain the tension. Delaying a capture can preserve options and make it harder for the opponent to settle on a plan.
